Data Mining on Sales Records of a Canada Retail Chain

2016, Dec 24    

Objective: The purchase choice of products of different customers

Different customers will show different preferences to products. To figure out that what kinds of customers will purchase what categories of products, this objective is going to use decision tree, including interactive decision tree and autonomous decision tree, and logistic regression to find the potential relationships, and moreover, which can help the company to set market strategies to different customers.

To build the decision tree and regression relationships, we need to define the target and Inputs first. The target variable is Product_Category, which is a nominal variable and has three categories, Furniture, Office Supplies and Technology. The inputs are used to define the characteristic of “Customer”, which include Membership, Customer_Class and Sale_Quantity. The details are shown in the followed table.

Model comparison

Objective_ Model Comparison

We use misclassification rate and average squared error to do the model comparison, and we find that Interactive decision tree is the best model with smallest indexes. Conclusion from Interactive Decision Tree

Objective_ Conclusion 1, 2

From a part of the tree, we can draw two conclusions. The first one is that corporate class customers, no matter the membership level or sales quantity, prefer to buy office suppliers; And the second one is that customers, who are small business class, premium membership level, and buying quantity is always large than 25 per transaction, prefer to buy furniture.

Objective_ Conclusion 3 And the third conclusion from another part of the tree is that individual consumers’ purchase preference is hugely influenced by purchase quantity. If purchase quantity is smaller than 5, they are more likely to buy technology products; 5-10, they are more likely to purchase furniture; otherwise, office suppliers will be first choice.

Potential Problem

Although we find the best model among the three models, the misclassification rate is almost 50%, which means it is very easy to misclassify and in practice, the value of the conclusions maybe quite low. The possible reason of the high misclassification rate is because that the inputs or called variables are not significant, which can be seen in the regression results. Therefore, to get better conclusion, changing the variables to re-define the customers’ features is a reasonable way to solve the problem.
